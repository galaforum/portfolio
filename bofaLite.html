Bank of America Lite

Role UX Research and UX Design
Team Size and Duration 1 member, 1 month
Tools Adobe XD

Background

The Bank of America Lite application is the lighter version of the original Bank of America application. It is a stripped down version that offers deeper functionality at the cost of memory space and device resources. It is an intuitive application that lets users manage their bank accounts and perform all basic tasks including viewing recent transactions, money transfer, managing their FICO score and managing their rewards. The possibility of getting lost and feeling confused while trying to do something in a heavy application with a lot of features has been the key motivation for the Lite application.

Need

The Bank of America application is very useful and shows most of the account details to the users. However, sometimes too many features confuse the user, especially new users and make it difficult to find the information they want. The application is intuitive but there is a need for a lighter application that solves basic user problems as not all users use all the features provided by the application. The idea behind a Lite application is originally inspired by Facebook which also has a Facebook Lite application which I have been using for a while. The original application becomes heavy in terms of storage space and provides a lot of features. The Lite application is small and saves space on the phone and also works well in sparse network conditions.

Research

I conducted a google forms survey on 16 people. I asked them to fill the survey about what features they used in the original bank application. From the responses of the group, I figured that they performed very basic tasks on the application including viewing recent transactions, money transfer, managing their FICO score and managing rewards.


Target Users

The 16 chosen people, in the age group of 22-28 years were college students or had just started their career in the industry. This group also had people who had started using the actual application a year back, so they were comparatively new users. Others had been using the application for a while now and were helpful in giving constructive feedback.

Learning Outcomes

1. User research helps understand pain points - User research helps undertand what users really need and what they want. It also helps to understand what confuses them and what they don't need.
2. Too much information chokes the brain - Sometimes, giving all the information at once may confuse users, especially new users.

3. User input is not enough - Correctly analysing user's needs is also important as sometimes even users don't know what they want.
4. Falling in love with the prototype is a pitfall - Getting too attached to the prototype results in overlooking faults. The key to a perfect prototype is to spend more time on the low-fidelity wireframe.
